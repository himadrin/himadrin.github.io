New York Times Explore

a highly stylized, visual application that gives subscribers a chance to immerse themselves in Times journalism all around the world

roles: Product Manager, Designer, Front-End Developer
technologies: Sketch, React.js, HTML/CSS, GoLang, Google Analytics, MapBox, NYT Article Search API, Google Maps API

Check it out here!

The goal of NYT Explore is to re-emphasize content and opinions that are less often on the front page, but equally as important. A user can use this application as a new way to experience what The Times is writing about by searching a location and discovering everything The Times knows about that location and its cultures. Editors curate the articles, images, and categories, helping keep the experience up to par with The New York Timesâ€™ elevated standards.

There are two ways to explore:

Direct search: Users can begin typing in the search bar and the Google Maps location api will autocomplete.

Interactive map: Users can click the animated arrows for the page to scroll to an interactive map. From here they can explore which articles are tagged where and click a location for more information. Each marker represents a tagged article and offers options to read the article or go to the location.

Both options lead to the explore page.

As the app was created as a proof of concept, we completed the rest with details and mockups as well as with a development plan to create APIs and tag to launch the app with full functionality.

Each section contains Journalist Notes, a list of articles (with the exception of Events), and a specialty feature. Events features actual current events that NYTimes editors deem worth going to, Food features NYTimes recipes, and Arts + Style feature unpublished images. Journalist Notes are a way for users to read personal opinions from journalists that would otherwise not be written in an article.
