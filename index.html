I'm Himadri and I'm a recent graduate of Dartmouth College where I studied Computer Science and Philosophy. Before moving to the great white north, I hailed from scenic Kansas City, Kansas. My favorite type of work involves identifying problems for users, strategizing creative solutions to those problems, and developing plans to tackle them. My background in research, design, and software engineering has given me the experience to understand and communicate about each phase of product development. I am currently seeking full time opportunities starting in 2020 in product management, software engineering, or digital media!

Most recently, I have been fortunate enough to work at the New York Times as an Android engineering intern on the App Platforms team aiding with the release, planning, and development of several new internal and user-facing features. I am currently at LookUp.Live as a Product Management intern on their web platform, where I am working towards a release of the site's redesign, and at The Dartmouth as the Director of Product Development, where I am spearheading the development of the paper's first mobile app.

In addition, I love learning new technologies and am currently working on mastering data analysis with TensorFlow and programming an iOS app to make me a Spotify playlist based on what I am wearing! When I'm not coding, I'm often dancing with my dance team, playing the drums, listening to and writing about music, reading New York Times articles, brewing coffee, watching movies, or making pasta.
