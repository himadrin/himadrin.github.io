I find it pretty hard to summarize anything, let alone myself but let's give it a go. First of all, I am a senior at Dartmouth College in beautiful, snowy Hanover, New Hampshire studying Computer Science and Philosophy. Before moving to the great white north, I hailed from scenic Kansas City, Kansas and yes, I am not in Kansas anymore. I have professional experience as a full stack web and mobile developer and find great enjoyment in learning from my teammates and mentors. I am currently seeking full time opportunities starting in June of 2020 in product management or data science in the fields of digital media, publishing, or music!

Most recently, I have been fortunate enough to work at the New York Times as an Android engineering intern on the App Platforms team, in a fast-paced agile development environment where I fixed several ticketed organization and user-facing bugs. In addition, I completed a refactorization of the Beta Settings module, which allows developers to add code to force app behavior, from Java to Kotlin while reducing lines of code by 45% and writing up documentation on best practices for adding settings. I am currently at LookUp.Live as a Product Management intern on their web platform, where I am working towards a release of the website's redesign, and at The Dartmouth as the Director of Product Development, where I am spearheading the development of the paper's first mobile app.

In addition, I love learning new technologies and am currently working on mastering data analysis with TensorFlow and programming an iOS app to help me manage my online shopping - most of my projects will be on GitHub, feel free to check them out! When I'm not coding, I'm often dancing with my dance team, playing the drums, listening to and writing about music, reading New York Times articles, brewing coffee, watching movies, or making pasta.
